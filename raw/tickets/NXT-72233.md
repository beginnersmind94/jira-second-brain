# NXT-72233 — Year-End Close — Post Closing Entries to Retained Earnings

## Description
When a user runs Year-End Close for a fiscal year, the system posts a single atomic closing entry that zeros revenue and expense accounts and transfers the net to Retained Earnings.

The closing entry is dated the last day of the closing fiscal year (e.g., June 30 for a July 1 fiscal year start).

This story posts the entries only. Beginning Balance, Current Balance, and Ending Balance calculation logic does not change in this story.

---

## Requirements

### 1. Trigger

The closing entry posts when a user runs Year-End Close for a selected fiscal year.

### 2. Posting Date

The closing entry is dated the last day of the fiscal year being closed.

Example: For a fiscal year of July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2024, the closing entry is dated June 30, 2024.

### 3. Account Coverage

The closing entry includes one line per revenue or expense account that has a non-zero balance as of the fiscal year end date. Accounts with a $0 balance at year-end are skipped.

### 4. Line Behavior

| Account Type | Line Behavior |
| --- | --- |
| Revenue | Debit the account for its existing credit balance, bringing it to `$0.00` |
| Expense | Credit the account for its existing debit balance, bringing it to `$0.00` |
| Retained Earnings | One offsetting line for the net (Cr if net income, Dr if net loss) |

### 5. Atomicity

The closing entry posts as a single atomic journal entry. If any line fails validation, no part of the entry posts.

### 6. Permanent Accounts Untouched

Asset, liability, and equity accounts other than Retained Earnings are not affected by the closing entry.

### 7. Posting Activity Log

The closing entry appears in the Posting Activity Log with a description identifying it as a year-end closing entry and naming the closed fiscal year.

Example description: `Year-End Close - FY2024`

---

## Defaults

* If a revenue or expense account has a `$0.00` balance at fiscal year end, no line is included for that account.
* If no revenue or expense accounts have non-zero balances, no closing entry posts. Year-End Close still completes; the audit log records that no closing entry was needed.
* The Retained Earnings line is omitted if the net is exactly `$0.00`.

---

## Edge / Empty / Error States

### Net Loss Year (Total Expense > Total Revenue)

The Retained Earnings line is a debit.

Example: Revenue `$1,000,000` Cr / Expense `$1,100,000` Dr → Retained Earnings debited `$100,000`.

### Zero-Activity Year

If no revenue or expense activity occurred, no closing entry posts.

### Re-Running Year-End Close

Year-End Close cannot be re-run for an already-closed fiscal year through this story. Reopening a closed fiscal year is out of scope.

### From Date Inside an Unclosed Prior Fiscal Year

Districts cannot start a new fiscal year without running Year-End Close on the prior year. This precondition prevents a From date sitting inside an unclosed prior fiscal year. No special report logic is required.

## Acceptance Criteria
### AC1: Net Income Year — Closing Entry Posted Correctly

Given a fiscal year ending June 30, 2024 with:

- Federal Reimbursement (Revenue): `$800,000` Cr
- Local Sales (Revenue): `$400,000` Cr
- Salaries (Expense): `$700,000` Dr
- Food (Expense): `$300,000` Dr

When the user runs Year-End Close for FY2024,

Then a journal entry posts dated June 30, 2024 with these lines:

- Debit Federal Reimbursement `$800,000`
- Debit Local Sales `$400,000`
- Credit Salaries `$700,000`
- Credit Food `$300,000`
- Credit Retained Earnings `$200,000`

And all four revenue/expense accounts show a `$0.00` balance as of June 30, 2024.

---

### AC2: Net Loss Year — Retained Earnings Debited

Given a fiscal year ending June 30, 2024 with:

- Total Revenue: `$1,000,000` Cr
- Total Expense: `$1,100,000` Dr

When the user runs Year-End Close for FY2024,

Then the closing entry includes a Debit to Retained Earnings of `$100,000`.

---

### AC3: Zero-Balance Accounts Skipped

Given a fiscal year ending June 30, 2024 where:

- Federal Reimbursement has a `$0.00` balance at year-end
- Local Sales has a `$400,000` Cr balance

When the user runs Year-End Close for FY2024,

Then the closing entry includes a line for Local Sales,

And does not include a line for Federal Reimbursement.

---

### AC4: Beginning Balance in the New Fiscal Year Excludes Prior-Year Activity

Given Year-End Close has posted closing entries for FY2024 (ending June 30, 2024),

When a user runs a GL report with From date = October 1, 2024,

Then the Beginning Balance for each revenue and expense account equals the sum of postings from July 1, 2024 through September 30, 2024 only,

And does not include any FY2024 activity.

---

### AC5: Beginning Balance Inside a Closed Year Reflects Historical View

Given Year-End Close has posted closing entries for FY2024 (ending June 30, 2024),

When a user runs a GL report with From date = October 1, 2023 (inside closed FY2024),

Then the Beginning Balance for each revenue and expense account equals the sum of postings from July 1, 2023 through September 30, 2023,

And the June 30, 2024 closing entry does not affect the result.

---

### AC6: Permanent Accounts Untouched

Given Year-End Close runs for FY2024,

When the closing entry posts,

Then no line is created against any asset or liability account,

And no line is created against any equity account other than Retained Earnings.

---

### AC7: Posting Activity Log Identifies Closing Entry

Given Year-End Close runs for FY2024,

When the user opens the Posting Activity Log,

Then the closing entry appears with a description identifying it as a year-end closing entry and naming the closed fiscal year (e.g., `Year-End Close - FY2024`).

---

### AC8: Atomic Posting

Given Year-End Close runs for FY2024,

When any line in the closing entry fails validation,

Then no part of the closing entry posts,

And the user is notified that Year-End Close did not complete.
