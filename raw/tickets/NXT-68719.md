# NXT-68719 — Backend Calculations for Profit & Loss Report

## Description
This story deals with the profit and loss report that will be generated based on dev work done in Phase 1. Since we are only doing manual entries at this stage, the final report may look different.

## Acceptance Criteria
The Profit and Loss report has the following sections:

# Cost of Goods Sold for Valuation Categories:
## Valuation Categories are sourced from Item Management > Item Configuration > Valuation Categories
## The formula for COGS is COGS = Beginning Inventory + Purchases + Transfers In - Transfers Out - Ending Inventory
## The table has the following columns:
### Description: This refers to the name of the valuation category and is sourced from item management. It *does not* refer to any data source from financials.
### Beginning entry:  This will be inherited from Item Management, however if there is no data then it starts from 0. 
### Purchases: This refers to new inventory purchased within that period and is determined by looking at the amount involved in manual entries marked as Inventory – Purchase.
### Inventory Transfer (In & Out): This refers to transfer of items across sites within the district and is obtained from manual entries of the type Inventory – Transfer In (from Warehouse) and Inventory – Transfer Out (to Warehouse)
### Ending Inventory: After integration, this value will be sourced from Item Management. However, since we are not there yet, we will determine this with the formula below using transactions of the type Inventory – Usage (COGS):
### Ending Inventory = Beginning Inventory + Purchases + Transfers In - Transfers Out - COGS(Usage)
# Revenue Sources:
## The revenue table is split into two sections: revenue from meal sales and all other sources besides reimbursements (e.g. catering) and reimbursements
## Data for the first section will be sourced from manual entries of the types below and each will be its own row within the revenue table:
### Revenue – Catering
### Revenue – Breakfast A La Carte
### Revenue – Breakfast Adult/Visitor
### Revenue – Breakfast Paid
### Revenue – Breakfast Reduced
### Revenue – Lunch A La Carte
### Revenue – Lunch Adult/Visitor
### Revenue – Lunch Paid
### Revenue – Lunch Reduced
## We will also be providing percentage changes here. The columns needed are:
### Description: This refers to the meal types served by that district and is sourced from accountability (?)
### Current Period
### Prior Period: This will be zero if we are starting from scratch when generating data
### % of Revenue: This refers to this revenue category’s contribution to total revenue including reimbursements for the selected period
### % Change from Prior Period: This refers to the percentage change of that revenue categoriy’s contribution to the total revenue compared to the prior period.
### Year-to-date: Cumulative amount from start of fiscal year to the date the report is run
### Item % of Category: This refers to that revenue category’s contribution as a percentage of the total revenue from all revenue sources excluding reimbursements
## Data for the second section will be sourced from the following entry types and have its own row within the table:
### Revenue – Reimbursement (Breakfast, District)
### Revenue – Reimbursement (Lunch, District)
### The table will have the following columns:
#### Description: This refers to the types of reimbursement and is sourced from accountability (?)
#### Current Period: This is the reimbursement earned for that category for the selected period and comes from the manual entry
#### Prior Period: This is the reimbursement earned for that category for the priod period and rolls over from the previous period or is zero if there is no data for the previous period
#### % of Revenue: This refers to reimbursements' contribution to total revenue including the previous section
#### % Change from Prior Period: This refers to the percentage change of that revenue categoriy’s contribution to the total revenue compared to the prior period
#### Year-to-date: Cumulative amount from start of fiscal year to the date the report is run
#### Item % of Category: This refers to that revenue category’s contribution as a percentage of the total revenue from all revenue sources excluding reimbursements
# Expenses
## The expenses section includes two sections: the cost of goods sold and the cost of operation (Selling, General & Administrative Expenses including salary and wages)
### Data for cost of goods sold is sourced the same way as in the cost of goods sold section. For each valuation category, we will indicate:
#### Description: Again, this is the name of the valuation category as configured in Item Management
#### Current Period: This should be equal to the COGS value associated with that valuation category
#### Prior Period: This is the COGS value associated with that valuation category for the prior period
#### % Change from Prior Period: This refers to how much the COGS for that valuation categorty changed compared to the prior period
#### % of Expenses: This refers to this valuation category’s percentage contribution to total expenses including SG&A expenses
#### % change from Prior Period: This refers to the percentage change in that valuation category’s contribution to total expenses compared to the prior period
#### Year-to-Date: COGS for that valuation category as of the date the report is run
#### % of Category: This refers to this valuation category’s percentage contribution to total cost of goods sold of all valuation categories
### Data for SG&A expenses is sourced from manual entries with the entry types:
#### Expense – SG&A
#### Expense – Payroll
#### Expense – Other
### The SG&A expenses table has two sections (Sales, Goods & Administrative (SG&A) and Salaries & Wages) and will have the same columns as the COGS section: 
#### Description: This refers to the name of the expense GL account involved in the manual entries of the three types above
#### Current Period: This refers to the total dollar amount for that expense GL account over the selected period
##### If a manual entry has multiple expense accounts involved, each will have their own row within the table (if not there already) and be the sum of its dollar amount over all transactions within that period for each expense account involved
#### Priod Period: This is the sourced just like current period but refers to the same for the prior period
#### % Change from Prior Period: This refers to how much the expenses associated with that GL account changed compared to the prior period
#### % of Expenses: This refers to this general ledger account’s percentage contribution to total expenses including COGS of the valuation categories
#### % change from Prior Period: This refers to the percentage change in that expense account's contribution to total expenses compared to the prior period
#### Year-to-Date: Total expenses for that account as of the date the report is run
#### % of Category: This refers to this valuation category’s percentage contribution to total cost of goods sold of all SG&A expenses (including payroll)
### For the Salaries and Wages section, we will be repeating the same process but only using expense accounts associated with manual entries of the type Expense – Payroll
# Totals:
## Provide sub-totals within each category as well as at the bottom of each column in that table
## Indicate Total Revenue at the bottom of the revenue table
## Indicate Total Expenses at the bottom of the expenses table in part 3
## Calculate Net Income by subtracting total expenses from the total revenue
### Use parantheses to indicate negative values
### All dollar values and percentages should be rounded to two decimal places
