# NXT-70909 — Update Remaining Legacy “Prepaid Account” Rules After POS/Online Liability Split

## Description
h2. 

Updates the remaining legacy rules that referenced the single "Prepaid Account" (PPACC) following its replacement by POS Prepaid Liability and Online Prepaid Liability (NXT-70340). Covers returned checks, sales tax on prepaid consumption, account adjustments, and meal sales.

h3. Context for Developers

*Pooled Balance:* At consumption time, the POS has a single pooled student balance. It does not track whether funds originated from POS or Online deposits. Financial Oversight does not receive student-level source attribution for prepaid consumption.

*Design Decision:* All consumption rules are stateless — they always debit POS Prepaid Liability. If this causes POS Prepaid Liability to go negative at a posting level, the system generates a reclassification entry at the end of the posting job to sweep funds from Online Prepaid Liability. This follows the [Oracle Multifund Cash Pooling|https://docs.oracle.com/en/cloud/saas/financials/25d/fairp/cash-pooling-for-multifund-accounting.html#Example-of-Cash-Pooling-Methods-for-Multifund-Accounting] pattern (Intercompany Balancing Entries method).

Why this approach

* Posting engine stays stateless — no balance queries during transaction processing
* Journal is honest — consumption entries reflect what occurred; the reclassification reflects what the system calculated
* No dirty period cascading — reposting a prior period only regenerates that period's reclassification entry, not every consumption entry through year-end
* CN Directors routinely reopen past periods for USDA claim adjustments; a stateful waterfall would break the audit trail across all subsequent periods

## Acceptance Criteria
h3. Rule 1 — Returned Checks (POS-only; Automated First Leg)

||Field||Value||
|*Name*|Returned Check – POS Liability|
|*Backend Code*|{{RTNCHCKPOS}}|
|*Rule*|Debit POS Prepaid Liability; Credit Returned Checks (Liability)|

Checks are physical instruments — POS only, no online check flow. The original deposit credited POS Prepaid Liability, so the reversal debits the same account.

{quote}*Trigger:* The returned check reversal is logged by a district user in the back-office account management module, not at the POS terminal. The engineering team should attach the system listener to account management, not expect a POS transaction payload.{quote}

{quote}*Second leg:* Debit Returned Checks Liability → Credit Cash in Bank is a manual journal entry, scoped to the manual/template entries story.{quote}

h3. Rule 2 — Sales Tax on Prepaid Consumption (POS-only)

||Field||Value||
|*Name*|Sales Tax on Prepaid Payment|
|*Backend Code*|{{TAXCLCTDPP}}|
|*Rule*|Debit POS Prepaid Liability; Credit Sales Tax Owed|

Sales tax is collected only at POS (confirmed in refinement). Always debits POS Prepaid Liability. If this contributes to a negative balance, the reclassification in Rule 4 corrects it.

{quote}Previously proposed {{TAXCLCTDPPONL}} (Online) rule removed — no online sales tax flow exists.{quote}

----

h3. Rule 3 — Account Adjustments (Manual; User Selects Data Point)

||Field||Value||
|*Name (POS)*|Account Adjustment – POS Liability|
|*Backend Code (POS)*|{{ACCTADJPOS}}|
|*Name (Online)*|Account Adjustment – Online Liability|
|*Backend Code (Online)*|{{ACCTADJONL}}|
|*Rule*|Debit Account Adjustments (Expense); Credit POS Prepaid Liability *or* Online Prepaid Liability|

Manual entries — the user selects which data point.

* *Positive adjustment* (adding funds): Debit Account Adjustments (Expense); Credit the selected Prepaid Liability data point.
* *Negative adjustment* (reducing funds): Debit the selected Prepaid Liability data point; Credit Account Adjustments (Expense). Accounts reverse; all amounts are positive values.

Note: Manual adjustments affect the ending ledger balance that the reclassification reads. For example, if a CN Director manually reduces Online Prepaid Liability by $5,000 in December, January's reclassification will see that reduced balance.

h3. Rule 4 — Meal Sales with Period-End Reclassification

||Field||Value||
|*Name*|Meal Sale|
|*Backend Code*|{{MEALSLS}}|
|*Reclassification Code*|{{PREPLIAB-RECLASS}}|

*Step 1 — Meal Sales Entry (stateless, per transaction aggregation):*

* Debit *Cash in Bank* — cash-paid portion (as reported by POS; never modified by Financial Oversight)
* Debit *POS Prepaid Liability* — full prepaid portion used (not split by funding source)
* Credit *Meal Sales Revenue* — total excluding sales tax
* Unused debit components may be $0

*Step 2 — Reclassification (system-generated, runs once per posting level per period):*

After all deposit rules (NXT-70340) and all consumption rules (Rules 1, 2, and Step 1 above) have completed for a posting level and period:

# Read the *ending ledger balance* of POS Prepaid Liability at that posting level. This is the cumulative balance including all prior periods, all automated postings in the current period, and any manual adjustments (Rule 3) already posted. It is not limited to net activity from the current posting job.
# If zero or positive: no entry generated.
# If negative: generate one entry — Debit Online Prepaid Liability, Credit POS Prepaid Liability. *The amount is capped at the ending ledger balance of Online Prepaid Liability at the same posting level* — never the full absolute value of the negative.
# Any remaining negative balance on POS Prepaid Liability after reclassification is real — it reflects student meal debt (charge meals permitted under USDA regulations and state anti-lunch-shaming laws). This is accurate and expected. The CN Director must reconcile this through their normal debt collection process. *This story does not create or post to a separate accounts receivable or student debt data point.*

Entry description: {{Prepaid Liability Reclassification | [Period Month Year]}}

The reclassification entry does not appear in the template entries dropdown — it is system-generated only and must be visually distinguishable in the journal and Posting Activity Log.

*Reposting:* If a prior period is reposted, only that period's reclassification entry is regenerated based on the recalculated ending ledger balances. Consumption entries in that period are stateless and do not change. Subsequent periods' reclassification entries may also need regeneration since they read cumulative balances — but their consumption entries remain untouched.

*Example A — Online covers the shortfall:*

Lincoln Elementary, January 2026. After all deposit and consumption rules have posted:

||Account||Balance Before Reclass||
|POS Prepaid Liability|-$1,200|
|Online Prepaid Liability|+$4,800|

System generates:

||Entry||Debit||Credit||Amount||
|Prepaid Liability Reclassification | Jan 2026|Online Prepaid Liability|POS Prepaid Liability|$1,200|

||Account||Balance After Reclass||
|POS Prepaid Liability|$0|
|Online Prepaid Liability|+$3,600|

*Example B — Online cannot fully cover the shortfall (charge meals):*

Washington Middle School, January 2026. High charge meal volume this period:

||Account||Balance Before Reclass||
|POS Prepaid Liability|-$3,000|
|Online Prepaid Liability|+$800|

Sweep is capped at Online's available balance of $800:

||Entry||Debit||Credit||Amount||
|Prepaid Liability Reclassification | Jan 2026|Online Prepaid Liability|POS Prepaid Liability|$800|

||Account||Balance After Reclass||
|POS Prepaid Liability|-$2,200|
|Online Prepaid Liability|$0|

The remaining -$2,200 on POS Prepaid Liability is student meal debt — real, expected, and the CN Director's responsibility to reconcile. The meal sales entries themselves were never modified — they recorded exactly what the POS reported.

h2. Applies to All Rules

* *Aggregation:* One entry per configured site level (Enrollment Site or Central Office, per district setting), per Period. Meal sales follow the same site-level flag as deposit rules.
* *Execution Order:* Deposits (NXT-70340) → Consumption (Rules 1, 2, 4 Step 1) → Reclassification (Rule 4 Step 2). Account Adjustments (Rule 3) are manual and do not participate in the automated posting job.
* *Business Rules Drawers:* New rules must reflect in the business rules drawers of associated data points.
* *Legacy Cleanup:* "Prepaid Account" (PPACC) is no longer referenced in active posting rules, business rules drawers, dropdowns, template entries, or new configuration/UI surfaces. Historical journal entries and migration artifacts are not in scope.
* *Template Entries:* Dropdown updated to reflect these changes. {{PREPLIAB-RECLASS}} excluded from dropdown.
