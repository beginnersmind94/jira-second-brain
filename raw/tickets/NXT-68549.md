# NXT-68549 — Fin - Generate Journal Excel export for selected posted date range

## Description
When the user runs the Journal report (separate screen) and clicks *Export to Excel*, the system must generate an {{.xlsx}} workbook that contains *one worksheet per journal* included in the result set, using the *Journal Template* layout we defined:

* Journal-level summary at the top
* Detailed journal lines in a table below
* Per-journal totals for debits and credits.

Parameter selection (posted date from/to, journal filters, etc.) is defined in another story.

## Acceptance Criteria
----

* *Workbook and sheets*
** System must generate a single {{.xlsx}} file per export action.
** All transactions should be in one sheet.
** Sheet name must follow this pattern: {{Jrn_<JournalID>}} (example: {{Jrn_102345}}), truncated as needed to fit Excel’s 31-character sheet name limit.

----

* *Header section (per journal sheet)*
Layout must follow the *Journal Template*.
** *Title row*
*** Row 2 must contain a merged title across columns A–I:
**** {{Journal – [Posted Date (From)] to [Posted Date (To)] for [Location]}}.
*** {{[Posted Date (From)]}}, {{[Posted Date (To)]}}, and {{[Location]}} values must be taken directly from the UI parameters used to generate the export. 
** *Journal info block (A3:B7)*
*** A3: label {{Report}} | B3: value {{Journal}}.
*** A4: label {{Location}} | B4: location name (from UI parameter).
*** A5: label {{Journal Date}} | B5: journal header date (date of export)
*** -A6: label Fiscal Year | B6: fiscal year label for the journal.-
** *Range & totals block (D3:E6)*
*** D3: label {{Posted Date (From)}} | E3: posted date from (UI parameter).
*** D4: label {{Posted Date (To)}} | E4: posted date to (UI parameter).
*** D5: label {{Total Debits (for this report)}} | E5: sum of Debit amounts for all exported lines of this journal.
*** D6: label {{Total Credits (for this report)}} | E6: sum of Credit amounts for all exported lines of this journal.
*** {{Total Debits (for this report)}} and {{Total Credits (for this report)}} must reflect only the lines of that journal that are included in this export after applying the posted date filter.

----

* *Journal lines table (per journal sheet)*
** Row 10 must contain the section label:
*** A10: value {{Journal Lines}}.
**** This label may be merged across A10:I10 for visual clarity, but cell A10 must contain the text.
** Row 11 must contain the column headers in this exact order (left to right):
*** A11: {{Posted Date}}
*** B11: {{Transaction ID}}
*** C11: {{Account Number}}
*** D11: {{Account Name}}
*** E11: {{Description}} (manual entries for now only!) (show only first 20 characters, user needs to then click on cell to see full text)
*** F11: {{Debit}}
*** G11: {{Credit}}
*** H11: {{Entered Date}}
*** I11: {{Entered By}}
** All journal lines that:
*** belong to the journal for this sheet, and
*** fall within the selected posted date range
must be exported starting from row 12 downward.
** Sorting for journal lines must be:
*** Primary: {{Posted Date}} ascending.
*** Secondary: {{Transaction ID}} ascending (or the system’s internal posting sequence when Transaction ID is not unique).

----

* *Debits and credits integrity*
** For each journal sheet, the sum of column F ({{Debit}}) must equal the {{Total Debits (for this report)}} value in cell E5.
** For each journal sheet, the sum of column G ({{Credit}}) must equal the {{Total Credits (for this report)}} value in cell E6.
** If the underlying journal is unbalanced at the database level, the export must still reflect the true amounts:
*** {{Total Debits (for this report)}} and {{Total Credits (for this report)}} must be calculated from the exported lines without adjustment, even if they are not equal.

----

* *Date-range behavior*
** The journal export must support journals that span multiple fiscal years; the system must not block exports that cross fiscal year boundaries.
** Posted Date (From), Posted Date (To), and Location values displayed in the title and header blocks must always be taken from the UI filters used to generate the export.

----

* *Numeric formatting*
** Debit and Credit columns (F and G) must:
*** use a numeric or accounting format with exactly 2 decimal places; and
*** be right-aligned.
** Zero amounts in Debit and Credit columns must be displayed consistently across the product:
*** always as {{0.00}}

----

* *File naming*
** The exported file name must follow this pattern:
*** {{Journal_Export_<YYYYMMDD>_<HHMM>_<DistrictCode>.xlsx}}
** {{<YYYYMMDD>}} must be the export date in year-month-day format.
** {{<HHMM>}} must be the export time in 24-hour hours-minutes format.
** {{<DistrictCode>}} must be the district identifier used elsewhere in the system.
