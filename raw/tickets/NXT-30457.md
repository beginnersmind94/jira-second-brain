# NXT-30457 — POS - Dynamic Menus - Calculate Breakfast meals based on contributions not just item counts, update pricing

## Description
Here is the Breakfast rule.

A reimbursable breakfast is formed from ½ cup Fruit/Vegetable and 2 additional +items+ from Milk, Fruit/Juice/Vegetable, Grain or Meat/Meat Alt.  There must be 3 items on the tray.Where the primary issue come into play is that there are cases where a single selected menu item as served may count as two or more items on the tray. Examples are a plate with 2 pieces of toast is served as one item but counts as 2 items. Another example is a fruit smoothie. Even though it is one served item it counts as 2-3 items because it contains Fruit/Fruit Juice, and/or Milk. 

To Address how to handle that I created an Excel document that has tables of combinations of different servings of item that would form a meal. The purpose was to give examples to determine when a single served item would count as multiple items for the meal. The rule I used was if it exceeded the minimum required amount by a full required amount it was multiple items Here is the Breakfast table.

Minimum Amounts

*  Meat/Grain 1 oz
* Fruit/Vegetable 1/2 cup (1st Serving)
* Fruit/Vegetable 1 cup (Additional Servings)
* Milk 1 cup

Item Counts based on above serving sizes

* Meat/Grain 1 oz = 1 Item
* Meat/Grain < 2 oz = 1 Item
* Meat/Grain 2 oz = 2 items
* Fruit/Veg 1/2 cup = 1 item
* Fruit/Veg 1 cup = 1 item
* Fruit/Veg 1.5 cups = 2 items
* Fruti/Veg 2 cups = 2 items
* Fruit/Veg 2.5 cups = 3 items

 The Meat and Grain menu category and the Fruit and Vegetable menu categories are combined. Under OVS, a student must be offered at least four food items and must select at least three food items, one of which must be ½ cup of fruit or vegetables. Minimum serving size is 1/8 cup. Quarter and eighth cup servings can be combined to achieve a 1/2 cup or more serving. In the above example items that exceed the minimum required of 1 cup or 1 oz count as 2 items

## Acceptance Criteria
If Menu Items selected do not form a reimbursable meal they are charged as a-la-carte

Extra items sold are charged as a-la-carte.

When a reimbursable meal is formed the price is updated to the reimbursable meal price for the eligibility type of the patron and the menu items are considered part of the reimbursable meal.

Once a reimbursable meal is formed additional items can be added to the meal as long as they don’t exceed the maximum allowed.

Menu items can be removed from a reimbursable meal but may result in the meal no longer being reimbursable.

When multiple items contribute to a category for a reimbursable meal and one is a-la-carte the more expensive item is considered part of the reimbursable meal and less expensive is a-la-carte.

Adults form meals using the same contribution rules as students.
