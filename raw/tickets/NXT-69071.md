# NXT-69071 — Configure Payroll System Accounts and Generate Ledger Entry Templates

## Description
District users need a simple way to configure payroll-related accounts (manual entries) so they can quickly create consistent journal entries from the Ledger.

## Acceptance Criteria
* a new settings page exists: *Account Mapping → Payroll & Manual Entries*
* when the page is opened for the first time, the system creates these payroll system accounts (if missing)
** *Salaries and Wages* (Expenditure)
** *Benefits* (Expenditure)
* the page allows users to add additional payroll system accounts as needed
** required fields: *name*, *base type* (Expenditure or Liability)
** name must be unique within the district
* each payroll system account row shows a *gl account dropdown*
** dropdown is filtered to gl accounts with the same base type as the payroll system account
** dropdown shows gl account name only
* the page also shows *Cash in Bank* as the funding account used for payroll entries
** cash in bank is pulled from the mapping completed in the wizard (read-only on this page)
** if cash in bank is not mapped yet, show a blocking message: “map cash in bank to continue”
* once payroll accounts are mapped, the system generates a *gl entry template* used by the ledger “add new entry” flow
** template rule (for each payroll account): *debit = selected payroll expense gl account, credit = cash in bank gl account*
** example: salaries and wages entry → debit salaries and wages, credit cash in bank
** example: benefits entry → debit benefits, credit cash in bank
* ledger behavior
** the ledger has an *Add New Entry* button with a *Payroll Entry* option
** selecting payroll entry lets the user choose the payroll system account (example: salaries and wages, benefits)
** the entry form auto-populates the debit and credit gl accounts using the template rule above
** user must enter at minimum: date, amount, description, site (if ledger entries are site-level in your design)
* validation
** payroll entry option is disabled until cash in bank is mapped and at least one payroll system account is mapped
** base type filtering must prevent choosing the wrong type of gl account for a payroll system account
** no duplicate payroll system account names for the district
