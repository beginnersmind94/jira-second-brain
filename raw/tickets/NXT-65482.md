# NXT-65482 — Configuration -> Valuation Categories

## Description
*As a* district inventory manager
*I need* to view and maintain the list of inventory valuation categories
*So that* I can control how inventory items are categorized, valued, and mapped to the correct general ledger accounts for reporting and posting.

## Acceptance Criteria
* *Display Columns (Data is sourced from Item Managemnt > Item Configuration > Valuation Categories*
** Valuation Category
** Valuation Method (e.g., FIFO, Last Received Price, Moving Average, Replacement Value, Standard Price)
** Asset Account (GL account for inventory value) (this is blank and to be filled in by the user, make it a searchable dropdown of all GL accounts so they can pick the relevant one)
** Expense Account (GL account for usage) (this is blank and to be filled in by the user, make it a searchable dropdown of all GL accounts so they can pick the relevant one)
** Data Source (e.g., Local, State Level, Food Distribution)
** Edit and Delete actions for the asset and expense accounts
* *Business Rules*
** Every valuation category must have *both* an Asset Account and an Expense Account mapped before it can be used in postings.
** Changes to account mappings or valuation method only affect *future postings* and do not retroactively change historical posted values.
** Each asset and expense account can only be linked to one valuation category.
* *Filters & Sorting*
** Ability to sort by any column (e.g., Valuation Group Name, Valuation Method).
** Ability to filter by Data Source.
* *Validation during account deletion:* 
**  If the user tries to delete an asset or expense account linked to a valuation category on the GL Accounts page, block it and inform the user that they must first assign a different account to that valuation group 
* *Change Management:*
** Once a valuation category has any posted activity, its Asset and Expense GL accounts become permanently locked and cannot be edited.
** If a district needs to change the GL accounts, they must create a new valuation category and begin using that going forward; historical categories remain frozen for audit integrity.
** Provide warning “This valuation category already has posted activity, so its GL accounts can’t be changed. To use different accounts, create a new valuation category and move the items to it. Historical mappings are locked for audit integrity.”
** Provide this tooltip also at the time of assignment
