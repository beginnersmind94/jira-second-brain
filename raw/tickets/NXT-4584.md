# NXT-4584 — API DEV - AcctMgt - Accounts - Special Account Search

## Description
*User Story*

As a district admin I want to see information about a Special Account.

*Mockup*

None required

*Requirements*

* In Acct. Mgmt - Accounts, rename the ‘Status’ column at the top-right (currently shows ‘Student’ and ‘Adult’) to ‘*Type*’
* In that new ‘Type’ drop-down, include the an entry for ‘Special Account’, which should encompass the main Special Account types:
** Principal Account
** New Student
** Visitor
** Staff (if it exists in the DB)
** Prog Adult (if it exists in the DB)
* When selecting the Special Account account type, show only the following additional filters: Site Code/Name and Status
** Selecting ‘All’ should also still pull up Special Accounts in the grid.
* When performing a search, populate the search results with the following columns:
** Last Name
** First Name
** Patron Account ID #
*** Can be blank
** Site(s) 
** Balance
** Eligibility (Free/Reduced/Paid)
** Status (Active/Inactive/Deleted)
* For now, there is no need to show the ‘Account Details’/document icon for Special Accounts. Only the summary info needs to show in the search.

*Background*

* Special Accounts are typically tied to special charge buttons at the POS.
* Information about each account type:
** Principal Account
*** A student may not be able to pay for a meal one day, or may be given a free meal for performing well on recent tests. Or the principal may be providing a ‘thanksgiving day lunch’ for all students.
*** Meals are rung up at the POS, and a ‘Principal Account’ button is selected.
*** The meal is recorded against the student, and the funds to pay for it are transferred over to the student.
** New Student
*** A student may not be in the system yet, and may show up at school for lunch.
*** With no account to bring up for service, the cashier instead taps the ‘New Student’ button.
*** A generic ‘New Student’ account is loaded, with Paid eligibility, and the meal is sold against this account.
*** They figure the rest out later.
** Staff
*** Non-food service employees (such as teachers) who do not yet have an account.
** Prog Adults
*** Food service employees (such as cashiers) who do not yet have an account.

Visitors

Family members may sometimes show up, like on ‘bring your grandparents to school day,' and will eat at the cafeteria.

These people do not have patron accounts.

Instead, a generic ‘Visitor’ account is used, with Paid eligibility.

They may also offer to pay for their grandchild’s meal.

## Acceptance Criteria
Status column is renamed to 'Type'

Special Accounts can be viewed (NOT clicked into) from the search results.

Information pertaining to the special account is shown.
