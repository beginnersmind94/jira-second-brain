# NXT-70124 — FamPortal - Website - Remove certain references within reCAPTCHA for compliance

## Description
From [Google|https://security.googlecloudcommunity.com/community-blog-42/switching-google-s-role-with-recaptcha-from-data-controller-to-data-processor-6646]:

{quote}"_Starting on_ *_April 2, 2026_*_, we’ll be switching from acting as a data controller, determining how personal data submitted to_ *_reCAPTCHA_* _may be used, to a data processor, processing the data strictly for your use in the reCAPTCHA service. This change enables you to have greater control over how your data is used._"

…

*_What you need to do_*

*_Action required:_*
_Starting_ *_April 2, 2026_*_, if your website currently displays references to Google’s Privacy Policy and Terms of Use in connection with reCAPTCHA, you will need to remove those references from your website. See the_ [_reCAPTCHA frequently asked questions_|https://c.gle/AHvOEDkOw-Oabz21lcVPTWSdLl2JXDnzNpLhKtKffo4oayt11ykbDmNYuRJKTmAcllbjo1NvkZLNoiN34qNV84_lZ19zn7KtUztrTriMRrhmEt8stjajSUFyQSToldu1Vx2RWUtb0XVxsUVDHGo2ntk] _site for more information._{quote}

----



Per DEV:

We are using *reCAPTCHA v3*, and the default *reCAPTCHA* badge is currently displayed on the UI.

This badge automatically includes links to Google’s Privacy Policy and Terms of Use. Please see the screenshot below for reference.

 

!https://dev.azure.com/Cybersoft-Technologies-Inc/0d08063f-212a-4277-87f0-199e49d410e9/_apis/wit/attachments/0c780f0b-934b-4735-b1d8-3f184fce4cea?fileName=image.png|width=1483,height=747,alt="Image"!

 

To meet the April 2, 2026 requirement, we will need to *hide the reCAPTCHA badge via CSS*, which removes those Privacy - Terms references from the UI. There is no functional impact.

We can also add a simple disclosure line in our own Privacy Policy , such as: “We use Google reCAPTCHA v3 to protect our website from fraud and abuse.”

This will be a *1–2 pointer*."

----



Let's do those two things in this story:

# Hide the reCAPTCHA badge/icon via CSS. Additionally, add a line to the footer that the site is Protected by reCAPTCHA. This should persist.

Add a line to the end of the 'Information Usage' section of our privacy policy: “We use Google reCAPTCHA v3 to protect our website from fraud and abuse.”

!https://dev.azure.com/Cybersoft-Technologies-Inc/0d08063f-212a-4277-87f0-199e49d410e9/_apis/wit/attachments/88fd54a3-8c22-4b86-95cf-82f842f36a86?fileName=image.png|width=1335,height=306,alt="Image"!

Both changes should also have translations in our existing languages as well.

## Acceptance Criteria
