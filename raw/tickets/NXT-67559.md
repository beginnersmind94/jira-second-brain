# NXT-67559 — FamPortal - Research/Implement New Age Verification Requirements

## Description
For legal and compliance purposes, we will now have to introduce age verification to all of our apps, starting with the Family Portal apps. New laws in several states, including Texas on January 1st, 2026, will be in effect soon, so we need to fast-track a plan to accommodate them.

References:

[https://www.pillsburylaw.com/en/news-and-insights/app-store-accountability-act-texas.html|https://www.pillsburylaw.com/en/news-and-insights/app-store-accountability-act-texas.html|smart-link] 

Summary of the new laws:

{quote}*_Developer Duties Regarding Changes (Section 121.053)_*

_You must_ *_provide notice to the app store_* _before making any significant change to your app. A change is considered_ *_"significant"_* _if it does any of the following:_  

* *_Changes to Terms or Policy:_* _Makes a change to the app's terms of service or privacy policy._  
* *_Data Processing Change:_* _Makes a change to your data processing activities._
* *_Affects Age Rating:_* _Makes a change that affects the app's age rating._  
* *_Monetization Change:_* _Makes a change to the app's monetization features, including adding new advertisements or in-app purchases._  
* *_Material User Experience Change:_* _Makes a change to the app's user experience or functionality that a reasonable individual would view as material._{quote}

This seems to be more of a future requirement. I think we are good on this section.

{quote}*_Once you notify the app store of a significant change, the app store owner is then required to:_*

# *_Notify any parent or guardian who previously consented to the minor's use of the app._*
# *_Obtain new consent from that individual for the minor's continued use or purchase within the changed software application._*{quote}

This is the first significant requirement. On the Android side, we may be able to use Google's API for this as well as the above section in the future. Apple will have one ready soon(?) as well. Please research this.

{quote}*_Other Relevant Developer Duties_*

_In addition to the change notification requirement, the new law imposes other responsibilities relevant to your K-12 nutrition app:_

* *_Age Rating:_* _You must_ *_assign an age rating_* _to your application and to any in-app purchases based on the state's age categories (_*_Under 13, 13-15, 16-17, and 18+_*_). You must also provide the_ *_specific content or other elements that led to that rating_* _to the app store._  
* *_Use of Data:_* _You must use the age and consent information you receive from the app store_ *_only_* _for enforcing age-related restrictions, ensuring legal compliance, and implementing safety features and default settings._  
* *_Data Deletion:_* _You are required to_ *_delete personal data_* _received from the app store after you have completed the required age and consent verification process._  
* *_Access to Age/Consent Status:_* _The app store must allow you to access current information about the user's_ *_age category_* _and_ *_whether parental consent has been obtained_* _for minors, which you would then use to enforce restrictions within your app._  {quote}

Verify the above is already in place.



Key research/dev requirements:

* Look into connecting to Apple and Google APIs to use Age Verification.
** We will likely want to trigger this when a new or existing user opens the app the first time after we introduce verification.
* Verify we do not collect any data more than is required to verify age and that we delete it immediately afterward.
