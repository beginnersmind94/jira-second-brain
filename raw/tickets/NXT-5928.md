*Business Information*

!image-20200921-191540.png|width=839,height=598!

This is the published grid from USDA. Everything on it is math. 

* Federal Poverty guidelines (“the poverty line”) are established, and every additional family member adds a set amount to this value.
* Value changes depending on location - Alaska and Hawaii have their own grids, the 48 Contiguous States also has one. 3 grids total.
* Brackets are setup (Free and Reduced) and are a percentage of the poverty guideline for each household size.
** Free is 130%
** Reduced is 185%
** We’re going to allow districts to add their own custom brackets if they need to, beyond Free and Reduced. These 2 should be considered permanent brackets.
*** This will mean the creation of a custom Price Type / Eligibility for students to fall into.
* Guidelines are broken out by certain frequencies for easy comparison to typical income/pay schedules.
** *Round up while calculating the frequency rates below.*
*** Annual rate = federal poverty rate * percentage.
*** Monthly = (federal poverty rate * percentage) / 12
*** Twice per month = (federal poverty rate * percentage) / 24
*** Every two weeks = (federal poverty rate * percentage) / 26
*** Weekly = (federal poverty rate * percentage) / 52

*Requirements*

The work here may need to be done in System’s current IEGs page. If we can convert this over to the new templates, that would be great.

* Update the current Income Eligibility Guidelines (IEGs) page.
* Show the current academic year’s IEGs when first loaded.
** Default selectors should be the current academic year, and the State category should be set to Contiguous unless the region is set to be in Alaska or Hawaii.
*** If Alaska or Hawaii, then the default selection for State Categories should differ appropriately. Alaska = Alaska, Hawaii = Hawaii.
* Populate the Eligibility Guidelines area with a table/grid showing the IEGs. 
** Brackets (Free, Reduced, Custom if present) with percentage value shown.
** Rows - Household Size from 1 to 8
** Columns - Guidelines broken out by income frequency (math above).
* The grid should update if the academic year or state category is changed and Apply is selected.
* Add an option to the page called ‘Show error prone ranges’
** This will need to show the income value ranges that are within $1200 annually of each bracket’s yearly amount.
** Example:
*** !image-20200921-194027.png|width=1094,height=158!
!image-20200921-194048.png|width=1105,height=262!
** If the Free guideline for a Household Size of 1 is $16588 Yearly, then we’d show the range as $15,388 - $16,588
** Other income frequencies break that $1200 down appropriately.
*** Weekly = $1200 / 12, Twice Monthly = $1200 / 24, Every 2 Weeks = $1200 / 26, Weekly = $1200 / 52.
