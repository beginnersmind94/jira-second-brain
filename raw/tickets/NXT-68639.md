# NXT-68639 — SC - MT - Roles & Permissions Central Admin

## Description
As a Customer Support role user, I want to have my own central realm roles so that my users can manage children districts.

*Notes*

* This story covers which resources (pages) Central Users can see in their children domains
* It does not cover what they can access in their own domain

*Requirements*

* Add 2 default roles:
** Central Admin
*** Equivalent of SchoolCafé Admin
*** Default permissions to everything like SC Admin role
** District Admin
*** This is a sub admin role for users to be able to manage district stuff
* These 2 roles are not deleteable 
* Admins are able to edit permissions in them if they have edit rights
* 
* New realms that are Central districts should have these roles instead of the District roles
* Ensure that when creating a Central realm these roles are added and the others removed
** If Updating from the standard roles to these, copy the users from Director and SC Admin in the Central Admin role

## Acceptance Criteria
h1. 

h2. *1. Overview*

* Applies to the creation and configuration of *Central realms* (state or multi-district entities).
* Establishes *default Central-specific roles* to enable centralized administration and management of child districts.
* Ensures that these roles replace standard district roles when a realm is designated as Central.

----

h2. *2. New Default Roles*

h3. *Central Admin*

* Equivalent in permissions to *SchoolCafé Admin (SC Admin)*.
* *Purpose:* Full administrative control for managing Central realm operations and child districts.
* *Default Permissions:*
** Full access to all pages and resources available to the Central realm (same as SC Admin).
** Inherits full CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) privileges.
* *Restrictions:*
** Cannot be deleted.
** Editable only by users with *Edit Role permissions*.
** Appears by default in every newly created Central realm.

----

h3. *District Admin*

* A *sub-admin role* designed for operational management of district-level activities under a Central realm.
* *Default Permissions:*
** Access to resources related to district-level operations (Sites, Menus, Reports, Inventory, etc.).
** Follows the standard District Admin permission set but adapted for Central oversight.
* *Restrictions:*
** Cannot be deleted.
** Editable only by users with *Edit Role permissions*.
** Always created alongside the Central Admin role when a Central realm is established.

----

h2. *3. Realm Creation Logic*

h3. *New Central Realm Creation*

* When a realm is created and its *Org Level* is set to _Central_:
** Automatically *generate the two Central roles*:
*** {{Central Admin}}
*** {{District Admin}}
** Automatically *remove standard district roles* such as:
*** {{SC Admin}}
*** {{Director}}
*** {{Manager}}, etc.
** All new users added to the Central realm default to one of the Central roles (based on assigned permissions).

h3. *Updating an Existing Realm to Central*

* When an existing realm is converted to *Central* (e.g., updated Org Level):
** *Add the new Central roles* automatically.
** *Remove the existing district-level roles.*
** *Migrate users* as follows:
*** Users assigned to the {{Director}} and {{SC Admin}} roles are *copied into the Central Admin role*.
*** Preserve their existing resource permissions to ensure continuity.

----

h2. *4. Role Management*

* Admins (Customer Support role) can *view and edit* Central roles if they have edit rights.
* Central Admins can *edit permissions* within the Central Admin and District Admin roles but *cannot delete them*.
* Role names and default permissions are *protected from accidental deletion or duplication*.

----

h2. *5. Data Integrity & Logging*

* All automatic role creation, user migrations, and updates must be:
** *Logged in the History table*, capturing:
*** Action type (Create Role, Remove Role, Migrate User, Update Realm Type)
*** User who performed the action
*** Timestamp
** *Transactional* — partial updates are rolled back if an error occurs.

----

h2. *6. Permissions & Security*

* Only *Customer Support* and *Technical Support* roles can initiate or modify Central realm configurations.
* All Central role changes follow the same permission hierarchy used for district roles.
* Security trimming ensures that:
** Central Admins see all resources available to their realm and child management scope.
** District Admins see only district-level features relevant to their operational duties.

----

h2. *7. Performance & UX*

* Role creation and migration operations complete within *5 seconds* under normal conditions.
* User migration preserves login credentials and permissions seamlessly.
* The Roles & Permissions UI dynamically refreshes to display Central-specific roles upon creation or conversion.
