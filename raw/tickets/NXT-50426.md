# NXT-50426 — Reports - Districts

## Description
As a Customer Support role member, I would like to view reports on the districts we have created in one page so that I can use that information to manage our customers in one place.

*Notes:*

* The scope of this page is for ALL districts at once, not a single district
** It is like a global dashboard, used for managing all licenses
** Do not restrict visibility to just data for a single district

*Flow:*

* User browses to Reports > Global Dashboard > District Reports Dashboard
* User sees a KPIs, graphs and data points for Districts

*Requirements:*

* On Reports > Global Dashboard:
* Create a card
** Title: District 
** Description: View key data points related to districts
** On click: go to the District Reports Dashboard page
* 
* Add a new page to the resource catalog
** Title: District Reports Dashboard
** Put it under Global Dashboard
** Disable it from being managed in the Role and Permissions front end
* 
* District Reports Dashboard page
* These requirements apply to Reports > Global Dashboard > District Reports Dashboard:
* Create the District Reports Dashboard page if it does not already exist:
** Title: District Reports Dashboard
* 
* Add a back button back to the Global Dashboard
* 
* Filters
* Add filters section on top of page with the usual collapse button
* Fields
** District
*** Multi-select list
*** Default: If need to default, then select all; if can, no selection = All
** Status
*** Multi-select list
*** Default: Active
** Apply button
** Clear button
* 
* -KPIs-
* -Create a collapsible section-
** -Title: Active Districts-
* -Default show this section-
* -Add KPI graphs and cards as described here-
* -The Filters section updates the KPIs in the same way it updates the rows in the grid-
** -Keep the data in sync for the KPIs as with the data-
* 
* Pie chart
** Name: Districts
** Data: Count of real Districts by Status
*** Excludes IsDemo districts
** Pie sections: Status
*** Ex: Active, In-Active
** Show labels & counts in sections
** Show total count somehow
** See Pie chart link for an example
** Drilldown:
*** Not sure if you can change chart types, but if you can, then do the following:
*** Show a grid with District name and Creation date as the columns
*** Ensure user can go back up to previous level
* 
* Bar Chart
** Name: District Activity
** Data: Count of Districts added and removed by month
*** Excludes IsDemo districts
*** Added is measured by date created
*** Removed is measured by Last Date
** Vertical axis: Count
** Horizontal Axis:
*** In Level 1, show Years starting with first year we had a our first client until this year
** Bars in each time frame:
*** One bar = count of Added
*** One bar = count of Lost
** Include labels and counts for each bar
** Drilldown:
*** In level 2, change Years to Quarters
*** In level 3, change Quarters to Months
*** Show the same data, just different time frame
*** Ensure user can go back up to previous level
* 
* Funnel Chart
** Name: Sizing
** Data: Count of districts that have site counts that match the ranges
*** Excludes IsDemo districts
** Funnel Rows
*** 1 - 5 Sites
*** 6 - 10 Sites
*** 10 - 25 Sites
*** 26+ Sites
*** Example: Adams has 55, so would be in the 26+ row count
** Drilldown:
*** Show a grid with District name and Site Count as the columns for each district in that row
*** Ensure user can go back up to previous level
* 
* Security
** Customer Support, Global Reports, Technical Support, License Management roles can do anything
** Everyone else blocked

## Acceptance Criteria
* Page added to menu system
* Page added to resource catalog under Global Dashboard
* Page is linked to from Global Dashboard
* Backend roles mentioned have access to the new page and district users do not
* Data on page is accurate and loads in a timely manner
