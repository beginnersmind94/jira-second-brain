# NXT-24736 — POS - Dynamic Menus - Calculate meals based on meal components, update pricing

## Description
As a user of Dynamic Menus I need the POS meal service to determine when a reimbursable meal is formed based on the rules set by USDA so I can utilize Menu Planning and Dynamic Menus functionality.

There are different rules for NSLP lunch and SBP breakfast in Offer vs Serve (OvS). Also the meal patterns can be different between programs such as CACFP and summer feeding programs. All rely on five categories of food components, Meat/Meat Alternative, Fruit, Vegetable, Grain, and Fluid Milk. Reimbursable meals are formed by counting the number of contributions or in the case of breakfast, items served, to those components and a count of the components with full contributions. Servings of a menu item can contribute to one or more components. Contributions can be in the range of 1/8 of a component to 2 full contributions depending on the amount served as determined by the menu planner. Those values are in the Contribution Counts of the Menu Item and may be different for different grades. 

A food component is one of five food groups that comprise reimbursable lunches. These are meats/meat alternates; grains; fruits; vegetables; and fluid milk. Schools must always offer all five food components in at least the minimum required quantities.

A food item is a specific food offered within the five food components. For example, a hamburger patty on a bun is one food item with two of the five food components (meats/meat alternates and grains). Separate 1⁄2 cup servings of peaches, applesauce and pears are three food items that represent one component (fruits).

Under OVS at lunch, a student must take at least three components in the required quantities. One selection must be at least 1⁄2 cup (total) from either the fruits or vegetables component. Depending on the planned menu, the student may need to select more than one food item to have the minimum quantity needed for a component to be credited.

*The Five Food Categories*

# Meat/Meat Alt
# Fruit
# Vegetable
# Grain
# Fluid Milk

*For Breakfast the Meat/Meat Alt and Grain Categories are combined, and the Fruit and Vegetable Categories are combined.*

*The minimum requirement to form a reimbursable meal is*

* For Lunch you must have full contributions from 3 categories with one being Fruit or Vegetable.
* For Breakfast you must have 3 Menu Items served with at least 1 being fruit/veg
** If a menu item has multiple full contributions of the Meat/Grain, Fruit/Veg, or Milk contributions it can be counted as more than one item.

*Once a reimbursable meal is formed, items in the other categories can be added as part of the meal without being considered a-la-carte items until the maximum amounts of the items are served.*

*There are also maximum amounts of a category that can be served as part of a reimbursable meal.* 

* For Milk the maximum is 1 full contribution
* For Meat/Meat Alt, Grain, Fruit and Vegetables it is 2 full contributions

*Items served that are over the maximum contribution allowed are considered as a-la-carte items.*

ie. If there is a grain item that is 3/4 contribution you can serve 2 items as part of the reimbursable meal as one item is not enough to fulfill the contribution amount. However a 3rd grain item would be considered a-la-carte. 

*When multiple full contribution items are served in a category with one being considered a-la-carte, the more expensive item is made part of the reimbursable meal and the less expensive item is a-la-cart.*

The First 1/2 cup serving of a Fruit or Vegetable is considered to be a full contribution to meet the Fruit/Veg requirement. After that a full component must be served to meet the fruit or vegetable contribution. As an example for a High School lunch where a student takes a Fluid Milk, 1/2 cup of pears and 1/2 cup of green beans, they must take an additional 1/2 cup of Fruit or Veg to form a reimbursable meal. (a 1/2 cup of fruit/veg for high school is 1/2 of a contribution to that category)

There are 2 Items created in Item Management to set the Prices for Reimbursable Meals.

Item 1. Reimbursable Breakfast Meal

Item 2. Reimbursable Lunch Meal

## Acceptance Criteria
A Reimbursable Lunch meal is formed for lunch according to the USDA rules.

A Reimbursable Breakfast meal is formed for breakfast according to the USDA rules.

If Menu Items selected do not form a reimbursable meal they are charged as a-la-carte

Extra items sold are charged as a-la-carte.

When a reimbursable meal is formed the price is updated to the reimbursable meal price for the eligibility type of the patron and the menu items are considered part of the reimbursable meal.

Once a reimbursable meal is formed additional items can be added to the meal as long as they don’t exceed the maximum allowed.

Menu items can be removed from a reimbursable meal but may result in the meal no longer being reimbursable.

When multiple items contribute to a category for a reimbursable meal and one is a-la-carte the more expensive item is considered part of the reimbursable meal and less expensive is a-la-carte.

Adults form meals using the same contribution rules as students.
