--- turn 5 [agent/reply] "customercare@primeroedge.com" <customercare@primeroedge.com>  2025-08-29T12:14:07Z ---
Good morning,

We will not be making changes to the functionality for transfers or receipts where the warehouse is involved in the transaction.

Sites can not receive items that are not reflected as "Shipped" from the warehouse, and the warehouse must initiate their own Transfer.

There is a perpetual inventory for the warehouse that would be impacted if sites were able to add items that were not on their ship ticket... if all items in the warehouse are "committed" - then there are none (or no extra quantities) available for the site to be able to "add to receipt."

We do allow for a site to receive LESS than the quantity listed on the ship ticket, as there is an allowance for items being damaged en route... and the appropriate adjustments for those scenarios can be performed from within the warehouse distribution.

Hello Leah Anne,

It's not only about the "current" settings when working with the transfer process... if the item has EVER been available at the site being selected, then it can receive that item via a transfer.

Likewise, if the site were to select "All Items" when performing a physical inventory, every single item they've ever had access to will show up for counting.

In the transfers area, this helps when there are instances where districts need a site to have "once per year" access to something, and allowing it in the transfer process makes it so that they don't have to go change the configuration of the item to get it there, and then go change it back...  for the physical inventory, the "All Items" access allows sites to be able to enter counts for products that may no longer be orderable/receivable, but they might still have some on hand that needs to be counted with the physical inventory.

It happens most often for things that are used only at school events on very specific occasions. (Pep Rally's, Bake Sales, Grandparents Breakfast, etc.)
