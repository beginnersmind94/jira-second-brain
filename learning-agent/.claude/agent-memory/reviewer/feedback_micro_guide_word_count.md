The micro-guide template has a hard ceiling of 1,500 words. On the 2026-05-29 item-management micro-guide, the Writer produced ~1,890 words — 26% over ceiling — primarily due to treating the "Also in this module" demoted-features section as a full `<h2>` section with 6 bullet items averaging ~40 words each, and by writing Common Mistakes at full paragraph density rather than tight bullets.

**Why:** The plan explicitly warned: "If the Writer includes sub-mention items from the Demote list as inline prose rather than strict one-liners, the word count will exceed 1,500." The Writer did exactly this — added an 8th `<h2>` section ("Also in this module") that was not one of the 7 required template sections.

**How to apply:** When reviewing micro-guide drafts: (1) measure word count before any other check; (2) if over 1,500, fail immediately → route to Task 4 (Writer, compress). The most common overflow sources are: extra `<h2>` sections beyond the required 7, demoted-feature bullets written as multi-sentence prose, and Common Mistakes written at paragraph density. Direct the Writer to compress those areas first.

**Recurring routing destination:** Template violations from micro-guide word count → always Task 4.
