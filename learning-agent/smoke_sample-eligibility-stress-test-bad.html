Income Surveys — Staff Micro-Guide

Purpose

After reading this guide, you can navigate the Income Surveys grid, add a new manual income survey on behalf of a household using the Add New Survey wizard, and correctly choose between saving a survey as Pending or submitting it as complete.

Who This Is For

District administrators and survey processors who manually enter or process household income surveys in SchoolCafe. System-level configuration (enabling Income Surveys, setting income range type) is handled by district admins with access to System → Configuration → Settings.

Before You Start

  • Income Surveys must be enabled for your district. The Enable Income Surveys setting (System → Configuration → Settings → Eligibility → Income Survey) must be set to Yes or Mixed — not No (the default).
  • Your district's Income Range Type setting controls how household income is entered on surveys (USDA Income Range, Household Income, or District Income Range). Confirm this with your system admin before processing surveys.
  • If your district collects Race, Ethnicity, and Other Benefits data, the Show 'Details' step setting (ENAISBENE) must be enabled so that step appears in the wizard.
  • Special Assistance program descriptions and case-number format rules are set up under System → Configuration → Settings → Internal-Eligibility-Applications. Confirm these exist before entering surveys that claim Special Assistance.
  • You need the Income Surveys role permission to access the Surveys page. Confirm with your administrator if you cannot see the page.

Top Workflows

1. Navigate the Income Surveys Grid

Find, filter, and export income surveys for your district.

  1. Go to Eligibility → Forms → Surveys. The page is titled Income Surveys.
  2. Use the Academic Year search field to select the school year. The current academic year is selected by default; the last 10 years are available.
  3. Switch between the All tab (all surveys) and the Pending tab (surveys awaiting processing).
  4. Use column headers to sort or filter results. Hidden columns (e.g., Student ID, Grade, Site Name) can be toggled on as needed.
  5. To export, click Export to Excel.
⚠ Watch out: The Academic Year field does not offer an "All years" option — you must select a specific year. If a survey seems missing, check that you have the correct year selected.

2. Add a New Survey (Wizard Walkthrough)

Manually enter a new income survey for a household using the multi-step wizard.

  1. From the Income Surveys grid, click Add New Survey.
  2. Students Step: Search for and add the students in the household being surveyed.
  3. Household Step: Select one of two response types:
    • Special Assistance — select the applicable district-configured assistance program (e.g., SNAP, TANF) and enter the household's case number in the format required by your district's settings.
    • Income — enter Household Size (must be greater than the number of students added), select Income Frequency from the district-configured list, then enter the income amount or range.
  4. Details Step (if enabled by your district): Complete Race, Ethnicity, and Other Benefits fields.
  5. Contact Info Step: Enter household contact information.
  6. Comments Step: Set the survey language (default: English), confirm or change the Survey Accepted date (defaults to today; any past date in the current academic year is valid), upload supporting documents if needed, and enter up to 500 characters of comments. If your district uses USDA Income Range, an Income Status (Free / Reduced / Paid) will display here based on the household data entered.
  7. Review Step: Confirm all entries. Use the edit pencil icons to jump back to any step and correct information. When satisfied, proceed to Step 3 below.
⚠ Watch out: On the Household Step, the system only recognises two response types — Special Assistance and Income. There is no "Verified Migrant" or "Pending Reverification" option in the wizard.

3. Save as Pending vs. Submit

From the Review Step, choose how to finalise the survey.

  1. Click Pending to save the survey without processing it. You will be returned to the Income Surveys grid. A success message confirms the save.
  2. Click Submit to mark the survey as complete (processed) and advance to the Summary confirmation screen.
  3. Pending surveys remain visible on the Pending tab of the Income Surveys grid and can be edited or processed later.
⚠ Watch out: Once you click Submit, the survey is recorded as processed. Use Pending if you still need to gather information or obtain a signature before finalising.

Common Mistakes

  • Household Size too small. If the Household Size you enter is less than or equal to the number of students added in the Students Step, the system displays an error: "Size exceeded by students." Always count all household members, not just the students on the survey.
  • Wrong Income Frequency. The Income Frequency dropdown is populated from your district's configured Eligibility Guidelines. Selecting a frequency that doesn't match the household's pay schedule will produce an incorrect income range. Confirm frequency with the household before submitting.
  • Incorrect Survey Accepted date. The date defaults to today, but only past dates within the current academic year are valid. Do not leave the default if the survey was actually received on a different date — this affects processing accuracy.
  • Looking for the History icon in the wrong place. Survey change history is accessed via the History icon in the Details column of the grid — not through an Actions menu. This is available on both the All and Pending tabs.
  • Income Status not appearing on Comments Step. Income Status (Free / Reduced / Paid) only displays when the district's Income Range Type is set to USDA Income Range. If your district uses Household Income or District Income Range, this field is intentionally hidden.
