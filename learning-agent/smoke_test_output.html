Income Surveys: Add & Manage Survey Records

Purpose

After reading this guide, you can open the Add New Survey wizard in SchoolCafe, attach students, choose the correct household response type, submit or save a survey as pending, and manage submitted surveys from the Income Surveys grid — without re-watching the recording.

Who this is for

District onboarding staff and eligibility processors who enter income surveys on behalf of households in districts enrolled in a Special Provision Program (CEP or Provision 2).

Before you start

  • Your district must be enrolled in a Special Provision Program (CEP or Provision 2). Income Surveys are not a substitute for standard free-and-reduced applications in non-provision districts.
  • Income Eligibility Guidelines and Survey Ranges must be configured in System settings — income frequency and range options in the wizard are drawn directly from those tables.
  • Case number formats must be set up at System > Configuration > Settings > Internal-Eligibility-Applications before you process Special Assistance households.
  • Student records do not need to exist in advance — you can create new pending student records during the wizard.
  • You need eligibility processor access to the Eligibility > Income Surveys section in SchoolCafe.

Top workflows

1. Launch the wizard and add students

Open a new survey and attach every student in the household.

  1. Navigate to Eligibility > Income Surveys in the left nav.
  2. Click NEW (top right). The Add New Survey wizard opens on the Students step. A progress bar shows all seven steps: Students → Household → Details → Contact Info → Comments → Review → Summary.
  3. Type a first name, last name, or student ID (combinations work). Select the student from the 10 most relevant matches displayed.
  4. If the system detects other potential household members for the student you added, it will offer to link them. Add applicable siblings.
  5. If a student is not in the system, click Add New Student, enter at minimum First Name and Last Name (required), and click Add. The student is saved in Pending status with an edit pencil (to correct details) and a trash icon (to remove).
  6. Click Next to proceed to the Household step.
⚠️ Watch out: Income Surveys do not require a categorical question or income question on individual students. These fields are handled at the household level — do not look for them here.

2. Complete the Household step

Declare how the household qualifies — this is the most consequential step in the wizard.

  1. Select one of two mutually exclusive Response Types (no default is selected; you cannot advance without choosing one).
  2. Special Assistance path: choose the program (e.g., SNAP, TANF, foster care) from the Case Type dropdown, then enter the Case Number in your district's required format.
  3. Income path: enter Household Size, pick a Frequency from the dropdown (options come from your district's eligibility guidelines), then select an Income Range.
  4. Continue through Steps 3–5 (Details, Contact Info, Comments) entering phone, email, address, and any notes from the paper form.
⚠️ Watch out: Choosing the wrong response type invalidates the entire survey. If the household has SNAP, TANF, or foster care, you must select Special Assistance — not Income. Picking Income for a SNAP household means the survey must be deleted and re-entered from scratch.

3. Review and submit (or save as pending)

Verify all entries and finalize — or save as a draft for a colleague to review.

  1. On the Review step, read through every section. Click the edit pencil inside any section to jump back to that step and correct it.
  2. Choose your action at the bottom:
    • Submit — finalizes the survey and advances to the Summary step.
    • Mark as Pending — saves a draft and returns you to the grid with a success message.
  3. After submitting, the Summary step shows a confirmation and an option to print or export to PDF [TO VERIFY — AC marked this feature as "(if possible)/(negotiable)"]. Use Add Another Survey to start fresh, or I'm Done for Now to return to the grid.
⚠️ Watch out: If you are unsure about the response type, click Mark as Pending — do not submit. Once submitted, you must use grid-level actions (Workflow 4) to revert the survey, and each action requires a written comment.

4. Manage submitted surveys from the grid

Reprocess, revert, delete, or restore a survey after it has been submitted.

  1. From the Income Surveys grid, find the survey and click Details.
  2. Select the action you need:
    • Process — marks the survey as processed (available unless the survey is in Deleted status).
    • Make Pending — reverts to Pending (available for Pending, Notified, or Processed surveys).
    • Delete — removes the survey (available for Processed, Notified, or Pending surveys).
    • Restore — reinstates a Deleted survey back to Pending status.
  3. In the prompt that appears, enter a comment explaining the change. The action will not execute without a comment.
⚠️ Watch out: All four grid actions (Process, Make Pending, Delete, Restore) are gated behind a mandatory comment. There is no bypass — have your reason for the change written before clicking the action button.

Common mistakes

  • Wrong response type for SNAP/TANF/foster-care households. These must use Special Assistance. Selecting Income for a SNAP household invalidates the survey entirely.
  • Looking for per-student income or categorical fields. Income Surveys collect income data at the household level — there are no such fields on the Students step.
  • Submitting when uncertain. Once submitted, reverting requires a grid action plus a mandatory comment. Use Mark as Pending on the Review step whenever you are unsure.
  • Missing household siblings. When you add the first student, check the system's sibling suggestions. Skipping linked household members means those students need separate surveys later.
  • No comment ready for grid actions. Process, Make Pending, Delete, and Restore all require a comment before executing. Prepare your rationale before clicking.
