**[00:15]** Rachel: Today we're going through the advanced Income Survey
configuration. There's a lot of detail here, so I'll move fast.

**[00:45]** Rachel: First thing — make sure your district has enabled the
**Advanced Wizard Mode** under Eligibility → System Settings → Advanced
Configuration → Wizard Behavior. The toggle is called "Enable Step Skipping
for Provision Districts" and the default is OFF. If you don't enable it,
the wizard forces all seven steps even when you only need three.

**[01:30]** Rachel: Once that's on, you'll see a new button labeled
**"Quick Survey"** next to the regular NEW button at the top of the grid.
Quick Survey lets you do a 3-step survey: Students, Household, Submit.

**[02:00]** Rachel: For Quick Survey, the system supports up to **47
students per survey**. If you try to add a 48th, you'll get error code
**E-451: Maximum cohort size exceeded — split into multiple surveys**.
Just make a second survey for the remaining household members.

**[02:45]** Rachel: Now on the Household step — this is where it gets
nuanced. There are actually FOUR response types in advanced mode:
- Standard Income (the regular one)
- Special Assistance (SNAP, TANF)
- **Verified Migrant** (for districts with the Migrant Education Program)
- **Pending Reverification** (for households flagged in last year's audit)

The Verified Migrant path uses a separate workflow — you'll need the
household's MEP enrollment number, which is validated against the state
registry through the **Backend Sync Service**.

**[04:00]** Rachel: One thing that often trips people up — the **Verification
Hash** field on the Review step. It's a 32-character alphanumeric string
generated by the system when you click Submit. You'll need to write this
down because it's not stored anywhere else; the auditor will ask for it
during the year-end Compliance Pull.

**[04:45]** Rachel: Another common mistake — staff often forget that
Income Surveys also feed into the **Accountability Variance Report**. If
you submit a survey for a student who also has a regular application on
file with conflicting income data, the system flags it as a "Variance
Event" and you'll see it on the Accountability dashboard the next morning.

**[05:30]** Rachel: For the Comments step, there's a **Required Topics**
dropdown that lets the auditor see exactly what kinds of clarifications
your team made. Three options: General, Income Calculation, Household
Composition. Pick one — leaving it blank means the survey can't be
processed by the **Nightly Batch Job** at 2:15 AM.

**[06:15]** Rachel: Speaking of the batch job — surveys submitted after
**11:47 PM Central** won't process until the following day's batch. If
you have an urgent submission, use the **Force Same-Day Processing**
checkbox in the Review step. That's a permission-gated control, only
district admins can see it.

**[07:00]** Rachel: For the Inventory of supporting documents — yes,
Income Surveys integrate with the Inventory module. Each survey can have
up to **12 attached documents**, and they're stored in the district's
**Document Vault** at /Inventory/Eligibility/Surveys/{year}/{district-id}/.
Use the upload widget on the Comments step.

**[08:00]** Rachel: Last thing — the **Audit Trail** view. From the
Income Surveys grid, click any survey → Actions → View Audit Trail. You'll
see every change with the staff member's name, timestamp, and a diff of
the field values. Audit trails are retained for 7 years per federal
requirements.

**[09:00]** Rachel: Questions? OK, we'll cover Eligibility Notifications
next week.
