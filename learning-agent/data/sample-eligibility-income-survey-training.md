# Eligibility — Add Income Survey (Training transcript)

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Presenter:** Rachel (Eligibility SME)
**Audience:** District onboarding staff
**Module:** Eligibility · SchoolCafe

---

**[00:30]** Rachel: Hey everyone, today we're going to walk through Income Surveys
in SchoolCafe. So Income Surveys are something districts use when they're enrolled
in a Special Provision Program — they're not the same as a regular free and
reduced meal application. Districts in CEP or Provision 2 don't need to collect
applications, but they DO need to demonstrate household income data to the
state. That's where Income Surveys come in.

**[01:45]** Rachel: Let me show you where to find this. Go to Eligibility,
then click "Income Surveys" in the left nav. You'll see a grid of all the surveys
you've already collected. Up in the top right, you'll see a NEW button — that
kicks off the Add New Survey wizard. Let's click that.

**[02:30]** Rachel: OK so the wizard has — let me count — seven steps. The
purple bar at the top shows where you are. You start on Students, then go to
Household, Details, Contact Info, Comments, Review, and finally a Summary
page. You can always click back to a previous step if you need to.

**[03:15]** Rachel: First step is Students. You search by first name, last name,
or student ID — combinations work too. As you type, the system shows the 10
most relevant matches. Once you pick a student, the system will actually look
at the rest of their household and suggest siblings to add. That's super
useful — saves a ton of typing.

**[04:00]** Rachel: If the student isn't in the system yet, you can click "Add
New Student" and that opens a drawer. First Name and Last Name are required,
the rest is optional. When you save, the student goes into a PENDING status
until the survey is submitted. Pending students get a little edit pencil so
you can fix their info, and a trash icon to remove them.

**[05:20]** Rachel: Question from the room? Yes — good question. No, you
don't need to enter the categorical question or the income question on
individual students like you would with a regular application. That's all
handled at the household level for surveys.

**[06:00]** Rachel: Next is Household. This is the big decision point.
Two response types here, and they're mutually exclusive — Special Assistance
or Income. Special Assistance is when the household qualifies through some
other program — SNAP, TANF, foster care. You'll select a Case Type from the
dropdown and enter a Case Number in the format the state requires.

**[06:50]** Rachel: Income is the other path. You enter Household Size,
pick a Frequency from the dropdown (weekly, biweekly, monthly, annual), and
then select an Income Range. The income range options change based on the
household size you entered — that's all driven by the state's eligibility
tables.

**[07:30]** Rachel: A really common mistake here: people pick the wrong
response type. If the household has SNAP, you must use Special Assistance.
If you pick Income and they're actually a SNAP family, the survey is invalid
and you'll have to redo it.

**[08:15]** Rachel: Details and Contact Info are pretty straightforward — phone,
email, address. Comments is for anything the household wrote on the paper form
that doesn't fit a standard field.

**[09:00]** Rachel: Review is the safety valve. Before you submit, you see
everything in one place. You can either click SUBMIT to finalize, or click
MARK AS PENDING to save it as a draft for someone else on your team to review.
Marking as pending is great when you're not sure about the response type.

**[10:00]** Rachel: After submitting, you land on Summary. There's a PDF
download button so you can print the survey for your records, and an "Add
Another Survey" button to keep going. The submitted survey shows up in the
main Income Surveys grid right away.

**[11:00]** Rachel: One more thing — you can edit a submitted survey by going
back to the grid and clicking Details on it. There's a Process / Make Pending /
Delete / Restore set of actions there if you need to fix something post-submit.

**[12:00]** Rachel: That's the workflow. Any questions before we move on to
Eligibility Notifications?
